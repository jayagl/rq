About ―

Rose Quartz festival of music & arts ~ 1st & 2nd Of April 2016

A sensory escape in the wilderness of southwestern Tasmania. Three days and two wild nights. Nature, scenery, isolation. On the shores of the vast Lake Pedder. Dancing, dreaming of a submerged crystalline beach.

A curated selection of the best musical and visual artists from Australia and beyond. And with no more than 500 tickets available, we’re keeping it cosy; split between 300 camping & 200 at the Lake Pedder Wilderness Lodge.


"Enter an endless subterranean cavern, where jeweled rocks loom out of the spectral gloom like marine plants, the sprays of glass forming white fountains. An immense multi-colored bird erupts from a bough over your head, flying off with a wild screech, aureoles of light cascading from its red and yellow wings. At last the storm subsided, and a pale light filtered through the stained-glass canopy. Again, the forest was a place of rainbows, a deep, iridescent light glowing from within.”

Location ―

Lake Pedder resides in the south west of Tasmania atop the Frankland Ranges, right in the heart of the Southwest National Park - Tasmania's largest National Park and World Heritage Area.

Originally a glacial outwash lake in 1972 before the Serpentine & Huon rivers were dammed, lake Pedder was expanded considerably from its original size to generate hydro-electricity. The original Lake Pedder was submerged along with its striking beach of pink quartz sand.

Rose Quartz Festival acknowledges Tasmanian Aboriginal peoples as the traditional owners of the land on which we are meeting.

Info ―
GENERAL
  • Camping opens at 10:00 AM April 1st with our Music program starting at 12:00 Noon
  • Music stops at 12:00pm Sunday April 3rd. Campers must pack up their gear by 1:00 PM. Lodge dwellers must check out by 2:00PM.
  • If you’d like to come earlier or stay a few nights later please feel free to book yourself in at the Lake Pedder Wilderness Lodge.
  • NO BYO - Rose Quartz Festival is an 18+ ticketed event, with wristband required for entry. Strictly NO BYO.
  • Don’t hesitate to send any enquiries through to info@rosequartzfestival.com.au
ARRIVING
  • Lake Pedder is located 2 hours from Hobart in the Strathgordon area. Our helpful RQF volunteers will help you once you’ve reached the site, designating either camping spots (if you’ve purchased a camping ticket), or directing you to the Pedder Wilderness Lodge.
  • Rose Quartz recommends taking advantage of our Shuttle Bus transfers from Hobart throughout the day.
  • We have a limited number of car park spaces available, so strongly encourage the bus ride. Tickets will be available shortly. While on the Shuttle Bus, you’ll experience the incredible scenery cruising in comfort with some aural AC. Get to know who you’re spending Rose Quartz with, while immersing yourself with the history of the site and some surprises along the way. Imagine sweet natural air, beautiful views and a stress-free entry into the festival. On the return, you’re able to unwind. We’ve curated a special experience for you.
  • Shuttle buses will be leaving from 10 am - afternoon in Hobart.
  • These will be allocated on first serve basis. And we have planned our Shuttle Bus service to match inbound flights from interstate.
  • Please follow our Facebook page for updates regarding tickets and schedules for the RQ Shuttle Bus service.

FAQS
General RQF Info ~

Q ~ When is Rose Quartz festival?
A ~ April 1st-2nd, exiting the site on the 3rd of April.

Q ~ Where is Lake Pedder?
A ~ Lake Pedder is located about 2 hours south of Hobart in Tasmania.

Q ~ Is the site wheelchair accessible?
A ~ We’re working on this with the site owners.

Q ~ How do I get to Lake Pedder?
A ~ We have organised a special Rose Quartz Festival Shuttle Bus service leaving from Hobart. We have minimal space for cars, and getting on the bus is an experience in itself, so we recommend it! Our friendly volunteers will help you once you’ve reached the site.

Ticketing ~

Q ~ How do I buy a ticket to Rose Quartz Festival?
A ~ You can buy a ticket through our website.

Q ~ How are my tickets sent to me?
A ~ You’ll receive a confirmation email from our ticket provider.

Q ~ Does my ticket include camping?
A ~ If you bought a camping ticket (and opted out of a Lake Pedder Wilderness Lodge room), then yes. The price is inclusive of accommodation and includes your ticket.

Contacting RQF ~

Q ~ Is there reception at Lake Pedder?
A ~ There is limited reception at the RQ festival site, making the experience during the weekend even more immersive. Put down your phone, you don’t need to tag your mates.

Q ~ How do I get in touch with Rose Quartz Festival?
A ~ You can send us an email at info@rosequartzfestival.com.au or send us a message on facebook via http://www.facebook.com/rosequartzfestival

Q ~ I’m a photographer and I’d love to get involved in documenting the festival. How do I get involved?
A ~ We’d love to hear from you. Send us an email using info@rosequartzfestival.com.au with a copy of some of your work and a brief summary of why you want to shoot at Rose Quartz.

Q ~ I’d love to review the event for … . How do I apply for accreditation?
A ~ Hey! Send us an email using info@rosequartzfestival.com.au and we’ll look into it with our festival directors!

What to bring ~

Q ~ Can I bring alcohol to Rose Quartz?
A ~ Rose Quartz Festival is a strictly no BYO event. We’ve teamed up with some amazing local brewers who are providing great drinks at affordable prices. No need to stress!

Q ~ What should I bring to Rose Quartz?
A ~ Pack for cold weather, Tassie can get really cold at night, particularly in the lush wilderness. If you’re camping bring along your tent, warm bedding and appropriate clothing for all weather.

Q ~ Can I bring a camping stove to the festival?
A ~ There is a total fireban during Rose Quartz Festival.

Q ~ Can I bring a djembe?
A ~ No.
